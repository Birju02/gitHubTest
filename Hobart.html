Introduction

From Launceston head south east on the national highway for 70 km. In this time, we will pass scenic routes, cliffs, mountain ranges and also water falls. Turning left on to Lake Leak highway and then in 50km turning left again on the Tasman highway we stop at Freycinet national park by turning left into Coles bay to rest and enjoy the beach. Then the following day we continue our ride head south west on Freycinet drive towards Jetty road and turn left on Tasman Highway, reaching Orford in 88km or 1 hour and 30 minutes.

History

Tasmania’s history trail begins in Hobart, the capital and economic centre of the state. To overcome the problem of overcrowding in British prisons, the British Government chose Australia as the location for a new penal colony. The southernmost island of Tasmania (known as Van Diemen’s Land at the time) was chosen for geographic reasons, but also because the British wanted to establish their authority over the island and prevent French invasion.

Local Attractions

Mount Wellington: Stroll through cool forested gullies along historic walking tracks or traverse Wellington Range in the saddle on horse or mountain bike. Four wheel drive along rough mountain trails, climb leaning dolerite towers, or sit quietly among the birds. Find the perfect place for a picnic or to flip a burger.

Salamanca Markets: Tasmania’s own market is a selection of your favorite Salamanca Market stallholders and is running each Saturday from 8.30 am to 3 pm. Our new look market consists of over 220 stallholders representing artisans, designers and producers - it’s as Tasmanian as we can make it.

Tasman Bridge: The Tasman Bridge is a five-lane bridge which crosses the Derwent River, near the central business district of Hobart, Tasmania. The bridge replaced a 1943 floating concrete bridge which had a liftable middle section to allow ships to pass through.
