Introduction

Prior to the arrival of Europeans, the area around Devonport had been home to the Tommeginne Aborigines for an estimated 30,000 years. The local Aborigines resisted settlement. This culminated in the killing of Captain Bartholomew Boyle Thomas, the district's first settler, in 1829.

History

Devonport is the first location in Tasmania we will visit, it is the ideal location to discover the north-west part of Tasmania as it contains majority of the historical sites. It has reserves, the beautiful Coles bay, an arboretum and a beautiful historic light house. However, this location is more of a layover, to recuperate and prepare for the rest of trip. From here it is about an hour ride to Launceston.

Local Attractions

Light house: At the mouth of the Mersey River, the Mersey Bluff Lighthouse is a red and white vertical striped beauty, set against the scenic coastal backdrop of blue skies and rocky shores. A popular destination for photographs, particularly upon sunrise and sunset. This historic attraction is the being of the journey in understanding Tasmania’s history.

Arboretum: The arboretum displays vast collections of trees and shrubs from the temperate world. Walking along old railway formation among local birds in season. Heritage animals may also be seen such as platypus
