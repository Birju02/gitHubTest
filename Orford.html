Introduction

From Launceston head south east on the national highway for 70 km. In this time, we will pass scenic routes, cliffs, mountain ranges and also water falls. Turning left on to Lake Leak highway and then in 50km turning left again on the Tasman highway we stop at Freycinet national park by turning left into Coles bay to rest and enjoy the beach. Then the following day we continue our ride head south west on Freycinet drive towards Jetty road and turn left on Tasman Highway, reaching Orford in 88km or 1 hour and 30 minutes.

History

The original inhabitants of the Orford area were the Paredarerme, also known as the Oyster Bay tribe. Historians estimate that they were the largest of the Tasmanian tribes.

Local Attractions

Orford Old Convict Road: A stone wall to the north of Prosser Bridge indicates the start of the old convict road.

Three Thumbs lookout: Located 7 km south of Orford is a two hour walk in Thumbs Forest Park, it starts at Three Thumbs picnic area and passes through dry eucalyptus forest and temperate rainforest. We will most likely have a picnic and ride up to the lookout rather than walking as we have long hiking trails already ahead of us.

Maria Island: This locations is only accibke by boat.Renting a ferry and taking in the amzing scenery from sunken ships and crystal clear water to vast coast lines exclusive to the Maria Island.
