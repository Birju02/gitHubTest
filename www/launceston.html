Introduction

This journey surpasses all the busy roads and takes us through the Tasmanian villages and farms stopping at iconic cafes and monuments. Starting our journey off by heading downwards on the river road along the Mersey River. Then left on Railing road observing the Tasmanian Trail. This trail is for walkers, riders and horseback riders. As we pass Deloraine we will cross the Meander River and enter the country side again where we can admire the historic village of Westbury. A worth café called the White House Bakery is a popular destination to stop by for snacks. We also have the opportunity to purchase high quality paintings from the John Temple Gallery and prints. As we pass through other country villages like Hagley, Carrick and Hadspen Launceston will be an exit from Westbury Road onto Prospect roundabout and following directions to towards Launceston City.

History

The 70,000 population of Launceston makes one of the highest populated cities north of Tasmania. It is located at the junction of the West and East Tamar, Tasman, and Midland highways, it is the headquarters of the state railway system. Launceston was first discovered in 1798 but was populated well before that. As we travel through Tasmania, it will become noticeable that there are more colonial ties present in the north, than the south. There is more colonial and Georgian architecture and roads built with Victorian gardens, however, many generations prior to their arrival, there were the Palawa tribesmen which were the aborigines of that land. Other tribes known throughout that region include the Leterremairrener, Panninher and Tyerrernotepanner peoples.

Local Attractions

Cataract Gorge, a 15 minute walk from central Launceston along the banks of the Tamar River into the Gorge

Holly Bank Wilderness adventures, Hollybank Wilderness Adventures is Tasmania’s only outdoor hub to house multi-adventures in one location, taking out Tasmania Tourism’s major tourist attraction award.

Harvest Market, Every Saturday morning in the heart of the Tamar Valley, Harvest Launceston Community Farmers' Market brightens a quiet inner city car park and brings the farm gate to you.
